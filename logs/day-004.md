# Day 004 — Penetration Testing Fundamentals

**Source:** TryHackMe  

## What I Did
Studied the foundations of ethical penetration testing — its legal
and ethical framework, the standard methodology, rules of engagement,
and the distinction between black box, grey box, and white box
testing engagements. Completed a simulated basic pentest as a
practical exercise.

## What Clicked
The black/grey/white box distinction isn't just about information
access — it reflects the *type* of threat being simulated. Black box
mimics an external attacker with zero prior knowledge. White box
mimics an insider threat or code audit. The methodology adapts
accordingly.

Ethics in pentesting is not optional paperwork — without written
authorization, the exact same actions that make you a pentester
make you a criminal. That line is a signed document.

## What Confused Me
The practical exercise was simple by design, but it raised questions
about how methodology scales on real engagements — specifically how
reconnaissance scope is defined and constrained in actual contracts.
Something to revisit later.
