# Day 002 — Networking Fundamentals

**Source:** TryHackMe Pre-Security Path  

## What I Did
Worked through networking fundamentals covering the OSI and TCP/IP
models, and hands-on use of core network diagnostic tools — WHOIS,
DNS, traceroute, ping, and dig.

## What Clicked
The OSI model isn't just theory for exams. Each layer maps directly
to where attacks happen and where defenders look. Understanding which
layer a tool operates on tells you exactly what it can and can't see.

DNS clicked as a recon goldmine — a domain's DNS records expose
infrastructure, mail servers, subdomains, and sometimes internal
naming conventions without ever touching the target directly.

## What Confused Me
The difference between TCP/IP and OSI in practical application —
OSI is the conceptual model but TCP/IP is what's actually implemented.
Takes some mental gymnastics to keep both in mind simultaneously.
